# The Biblion Manifesto

## Why Biblion Exists

Biblion exists to preserve, study, and share humanity's written heritage through thoughtful application of modern technology.

Its inspiration comes from a simple conviction:

Knowledge that has survived for centuries deserves every opportunity to survive the centuries yet to come.

For many people—including the founder of this project—that conviction begins with the preservation of Scripture. Throughout history, faithful scholars, scribes, translators, and humanists devoted their lives to preserving and transmitting texts so that future generations could study them. Biblion continues that tradition using the tools available today.

Our work is motivated by the belief that technology should serve scholarship rather than replace it.

Artificial intelligence is not the destination.

It is one of many instruments that can help historians, theologians, philosophers, archivists, librarians, linguists, and researchers accomplish work that would otherwise require countless hours of manual effort.

Every corrected page, every restored manuscript, every verified transcription, and every preserved source contributes to humanity's collective memory.

## Our Philosophy

We believe that artificial intelligence should extend human scholarship, not replace it.

AI can assist with restoration.

AI can assist with transcription.

AI can assist with comparison.

AI can assist with organization.

But understanding remains a human responsibility.

Biblion exists to place better tools into the hands of those who seek understanding.

## Our Commitment

Biblion is committed to:

* preserving textual heritage with integrity
* documenting provenance whenever possible
* making complex workflows understandable
* teaching responsible uses of artificial intelligence
* encouraging open collaboration
* building software that future generations can maintain and improve

## Our Invitation

Whether you are a historian, theologian, philosopher, librarian, archivist, software developer, student, researcher, or simply someone who values the preservation of knowledge, you are welcome here.

Every contribution matters.

Some will write code.

Some will improve documentation.

Some will test software.

Some will correct OCR.

Some will preserve collections that might otherwise disappear.

Together, those efforts become larger than any one person.

## Looking Forward

Biblion is not simply an OCR project.

It is an investment in preserving humanity's written record and making that record more accessible through careful engineering, open collaboration, and responsible application of artificial intelligence.

The tools will evolve.

The technology will change.

The mission will remain.
