# The Biblion Project

## Definition

Biblion is an open, interdisciplinary Digital Humanities platform dedicated to the preservation, study, publication, and responsible application of artificial intelligence to humanity's written heritage.

Its purpose is to unite modern computing with traditional scholarship in order to make historical texts more accessible, more understandable, and more durable for future generations.

Although its initial development focuses on biblical and historical literature, the principles and technologies developed by Biblion are intended to support the preservation of any significant textual collection.

---

# The Name

The name *Biblion* is derived from the Greek word βιβλίον (*biblion*), meaning "book" or "scroll."

The name reflects both the historical origins of written scholarship and the project's long-term commitment to preserving humanity's written record.

---

# Vision

Biblion envisions a future in which modern technology strengthens—not replaces—the work of scholars, historians, librarians, theologians, philosophers, linguists, archivists, and educators.

The project seeks to demonstrate that artificial intelligence can become a responsible partner in scholarship when guided by human judgment, historical context, and careful stewardship.

---

# Scope

The Biblion Project encompasses far more than optical character recognition.

Its long-term scope includes:

* document acquisition
* image restoration
* OCR and handwriting recognition
* textual correction
* provenance management
* historical research
* digital preservation
* scholarly publication
* workflow automation
* educational resources
* community collaboration
* responsible AI-assisted scholarship

Each capability contributes to a larger mission of preserving and understanding written knowledge.

---

# Guiding Principles

Biblion is guided by several enduring principles.

### Preservation Before Convenience

Historical materials should be preserved with integrity.

Whenever possible, source material should remain recoverable, traceable, and documented.

---

### Scholarship Before Automation

Automation exists to support scholarship.

Human understanding remains central to every meaningful interpretation.

---

### Documentation Before Assumption

Knowledge should never exist only in source code.

Architectural decisions, research, and historical context deserve careful documentation.

---

### Community Before Individual

Although every project begins with individuals, lasting projects become communities.

Biblion is designed to welcome contributors from many disciplines, recognizing that meaningful preservation requires diverse expertise.

---

### Stewardship Before Ownership

Biblion treats historical texts as a shared human inheritance.

The project exists to serve that inheritance rather than to possess it.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence is an important component of Biblion, but it is not its purpose.

AI is viewed as an extension of human capability rather than a replacement for human wisdom.

Within Biblion, AI assists with repetitive, computationally intensive, and pattern-recognition tasks while leaving interpretation, scholarship, editorial judgment, and historical understanding to human contributors.

Responsible use of AI requires transparency, accountability, and continual learning.

---

# Digital Humanities

Biblion is fundamentally a Digital Humanities project.

It seeks to bridge computer science and the humanities by applying engineering principles to the preservation and study of historical texts.

The project encourages collaboration among software developers, scholars, librarians, archivists, educators, publishers, and students.

The strongest solutions emerge when technical excellence and scholarly rigor are pursued together.

---

# Publications

Biblion is intended not only to process historical documents but also to assist in producing new scholarly editions, digital resources, educational materials, and research publications.

Future publication targets may include digital libraries, electronic study resources, archival collections, and printed editions prepared with transparent provenance and carefully documented workflows.

---

# Community

Biblion welcomes participation from anyone who shares its commitment to preserving and understanding humanity's written heritage.

Contributors may include software developers, historians, theologians, philosophers, librarians, archivists, linguists, educators, publishers, students, and volunteers.

Every discipline contributes knowledge that strengthens the whole.

---

# Long-Term Objective

The ultimate objective of the Biblion Project is not simply to build software.

It is to cultivate a sustainable ecosystem where technology, scholarship, education, and community work together to preserve humanity's written record for generations yet to come.

As technology evolves, the tools will change.

The mission will endure.

---

*Scholarship preserves understanding.*

*Technology extends stewardship.*

*Biblion exists where all three meet."*
